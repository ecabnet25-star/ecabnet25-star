## LINKEDIN — Post 2 (Tutorial angle — Amazon listing)

**Text:**
I've been helping e-commerce sellers use AI to write Amazon listings.

Here's the prompt structure that consistently produces strong output:

For the title:
"Write an Amazon product listing title for [product]. Lead with brand name, include primary keyword near the front, add 2-3 key features, keep under 200 characters."

For bullet points:
"Write 5 Amazon bullet points. Each starts with a bold benefit in CAPS, followed by 1-2 sentences of detail. Focus on customer benefits not features. Include these keywords naturally: [list]."

The before/after difference is significant. Most sellers write titles like "Stainless Steel Travel Mug 16oz" — which tells you what it is but gives Amazon's algorithm nothing to work with.

AI-optimised version: "BrandName Travel Coffee Mug – 16oz Double-Wall Stainless Steel, Keeps Hot 12 Hours, Leak-Proof, Fits Car Cup Holder"

Same product. Second one hits the algorithm signals and the buyer simultaneously.

Full tutorial with more examples: [link to ai-amazon-listing-optimisation.html]
